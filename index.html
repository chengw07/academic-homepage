About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at UNC Charlotte. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2015. Before that, I received my B.E. and M.S. degrees from Beihang University, China.

My research interests include data mining, machine learning, and their applications in healthcare analytics, social network analysis, and business intelligence. I am particularly interested in developing efficient and effective algorithms for analyzing large-scale, complex data.

Research Projects

Current and past research initiatives

Robust Graph Learning for Data Mining

Developing robust graph learning algorithms that can effectively handle noisy and incomplete data in real-world applications.

This project aims to address the challenges of learning meaningful graph representations from noisy data sources. We are developing novel algorithms that can identify and mitigate the effects of noise while preserving the underlying structure of the data.

Healthcare Analytics using Machine Learning

Applying machine learning techniques to healthcare data for improved diagnosis and treatment recommendations.

This project focuses on developing predictive models for disease progression and treatment outcomes using electronic health records. We are collaborating with healthcare providers to validate our approaches in clinical settings.

Multi-Network Analysis for Social Media

Analyzing multiple social networks simultaneously to discover cross-platform patterns and user behaviors.

This research explores how information spreads across different social media platforms and how user communities form and evolve over time. We are developing algorithms that can integrate data from multiple networks to provide a more comprehensive view of social dynamics.
